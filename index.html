  5. (Optional but recommended) Restrict the key to Cloud Text-to-Speech API only under API restrictions.
  6. Paste the key into the field below. It will be saved in your browser for next time.

·End sentences with a period — it produces a natural pause. A comma produces a shorter pause.
·Use an ellipsis (…) to create a longer, more dramatic pause between clauses.
·Capitalisation affects stress — all-caps words like ECG or CABG may be spelled out letter-by-letter depending on the voice. Use SSML <sub> to control this.
·Numbers are read as spoken words. "3" → "three". For ordinals like "3rd", write it out or use SSML.
·For a natural narration pace, write shorter sentences. Long run-on sentences with many clauses can sound rushed.
·Switch to SSML mode for precise control over pauses, emphasis, pronunciation, and pacing of specific words.
·Markup is a Chirp 3 HD–exclusive input mode. It's simpler than SSML — just write plain prose and drop pause tags inline where you want silences.
·Pause duration is contextual — the AI decides the exact length based on surrounding text, like a human speaker would. Results may vary slightly between generations.
·You can stack tags for longer silences, e.g. [pause long][pause long], but don't overdo it.
·Tags placed in unnatural positions (mid-word, inside a clause) may be ignored by the model.
